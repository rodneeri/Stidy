# Product

## Register

product

## Users

DUPLiiST is a desktop/web tool for migrating and downloading music playlists (Spotify, YouTube, SoundCloud → MP3/FLAC/M4A with embedded metadata and cover art). Originally personal-use, but built for wider distribution — it may be shared with friends and strangers who are not technical. Users come in with a playlist URL or search query, want to see their tracks resolve quickly, pick what to keep, and convert or download with minimal friction. Primary workflow: paste → resolve matches → review/select → convert or download.

## Product Purpose

Make cross-platform playlist migration and bulk MP3 downloading feel effortless and trustworthy. Success looks like: a user pastes a URL, watches matches resolve in real time, and completes a download or conversion without confusion — even on their first visit, with no prior explanation.

## Brand Personality

Sleek, techy, premium — should feel like a polished, high-production "$20k website" rather than a utility script with a UI bolted on. Dark, modern, with deliberate motion (splash sequence, interactive micro-animations, glow/gradient accents) and clean information hierarchy. Confident and a little futuristic, but never gimmicky — every animation and visual flourish should feel intentional and reinforce the "this is a real product" impression.

## Anti-references

- Generic "dev tool" look: bare forms, default browser styling, no motion, no personality.
- Cluttered or cheap-feeling effects — gratuitous gradients/glows that don't serve hierarchy or feedback.
- Anything that reads as a sketchy/dodgy downloader site (aggressive ads, dark patterns, confusing CTAs) — trust matters since this touches copyrighted content and user credentials.

## Design Principles

1. **First-run polish** — a stranger landing on this with zero context should immediately read it as a professional, trustworthy product, not a hobby script.
2. **Motion with intent** — the splash, progress states, and interactions should feel premium and alive, but every animation should communicate state (loading, success, error) or guide attention, not just decorate.
3. **Workflow clarity under the polish** — paste → resolve → select → convert/download must stay obvious and fast even as visual production value increases.
4. **Status legibility at a glance** — track states (pending, searching, found, error, downloaded) are core to the experience and must remain instantly scannable.
5. **Build on the existing dark techy identity** — refine and elevate the current purple/cyan dark theme and splash rather than discarding it outright, unless a specific change clearly serves the "premium product" goal better.
